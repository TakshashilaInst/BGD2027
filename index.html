About the Dialogue

An Invitation to Conceive the Future of
Technology, Innovation and Strategy in India

The Bangalore Global Dialogue will be India's premier independent convening on technology, innovation, defence and geopolitical strategy. It will be a unique forum where industry leaders, technologists, scientists, entrepreneurs, military leaders and strategic experts can brainstorm together to conceive the future of technology, innovation, and strategy in India.


The Bangalore Global Dialogue is not just another tech conclave, defence exhibition or policy conference — it is a festival of futuristic ideas and spotlights the Bangalore view on technology, innovation and national security. Disruption is no longer the exception; it is the norm. Old alliances are under strain, the multilateral order is fraying, and new technologies are reshaping economies, redefining warfare, and shifting the theatres of conflict from the physical to the virtual. The Dialogue will bring together leading voices to explore these shifts in geopolitics, geoeconomics, technology and ideology.

Programme Formats

A Mela of Ideas, Debates
& Discoveries

The Bangalore Global Dialogue is not a conference where attendees sit back as audiences. Every attendee is also a participant. Each format is crafted to tap into the room's collective knowledge.

Keynotes & Brainstormers

High-level addresses that set the intellectual tone, followed by research-backed position papers and interactive discussions that are both grounded and forward-looking.

Town Hall (Sabha)

Inspired by India's deliberative traditions. A host and expert ignite the conversation, but the floor belongs to everyone. Collective thinking in action.

Simulation & War-games

Participants, divided into groups, craft strategic moves addressing specific scenarios. Strategies are projected live, creating a mosaic of global complexity.

Purva Paksha Debate

Reviving the Indian scholarly tradition of articulating the strongest version of an opposing view before critiquing it — producing debates where participants listen deeply.

Tech Presentations & Demos

Styled like TED Talks — 20 minutes, sharp, powerful, and visual. Fresh, provocative ideas that spark conversations across coffee tables.

Reflective Conversations

Evenings slow down to explore histories, ideas, and philosophies — situating today's challenges in the sweep of history with civilisational depth.

About the Organiser

The Takshashila Institution

An independent centre for research and education in public policy. Non-partisan, non-profit — advocating freedom, openness, tolerance, pluralism, and responsible citizenship.

Policy Education

Graduate certificate programmes in Advanced Public Policy, Defence & Foreign Affairs, and Technology Policy. Over 8,000 alumni across four continents including professionals, academics, civic leaders, and government officials.

Policy Research

Research spanning Advanced Biology, High-Tech Geopolitics, Strategic Studies, Advanced Military Technologies, Geospatial Research, Indo-Pacific Studies, and Economic Policy. Over 40 research staff in Bangalore and a network of 100+ adjunct scholars worldwide.

Policy Media

Takshashila hosts workshops, roundtable discussions & collaborative events on areas related to its research. It also has nine in-house newsletters covering various topics and three podcasts, including All Things Policy, Great Powers Show, and Puliyabaazi.
